**Hypothesis: Confidence-Graded Knowledge Retrieval**

AI-generated changelogs may contain varying levels of speculation presented as fact. We propose that chunk metadata could enable confidence assessment through:

**Authority Correlation:** User conversation similarity might indicate validated versus AI-inferred decisions. Chunks with high user message semantic overlap could receive higher confidence scores.

**Evidence Patterns:** Chunks lacking genealogical links, continuation, or contextual support might represent isolated speculation requiring validation flags.

**Temporal Validation:** Claims could gain confidence through sustained non-contradiction, while aging unverified assertions might warrant uncertainty indicators.

**Graduated Serving:** Template rendering could reflect confidence levels - from confirmed approaches to suggested implementations requiring validation.

This approach might distinguish user-driven decisions from AI interpretations, potentially improving knowledge reliability without discarding valuable but uncertain insights.

The hypothesis suggests our rich metadata (genealogy, temporal tracking, structural completeness) could enable honest uncertainty communication rather than uniform confident presentation.
